Audience

Internship Recruiters - Based on experience interacting with internship recruiters, I know they typically look for some sort of project process. They want to know what the problem was, how you went about obtaining a solution, and what you learned and decided along the way. To make this site successful to them, I need to show the finished product as well as how I got there. They also would be interested in my professional history and education experience, so a resume would be helpful.

Professors - Based on things professors have said in the past, they like to see a lot of things similar to recruiters. They are also interested in learning a bit about me, whether that be as a student or just an individual. What do I do with my time outside of classes? What are my hobbies? Am I involved in anything else? They may not be as concerned with a resume, but they definitely are still interested in seeing my work.

Based on what I’m looking for from other peoples’ portfolios, peers would typically be looking for my projects, and how I present them. Underclassmen would be looking for my solutions to the projects they’re doing in class, and they want to know what I did to get to that point.

Design

I've attached my sketches above which outline my two column grid approach to desktop. When choosing design elements, I chose colors that I thought were engaging and reflected my personality. The type face I wanted to be bold and modern, but still clean, and the photos for the projects were meant to give a little preview of what the project was but not give the whole thing away just yet. I wanted them to be a little window into the project, but not the whole thing.

Resources

I coded all of this myself except for the hamburger menu. I followed a tutorial to help me get the functionality working , but then changed the visual elements. I changed colors, size of the text added hover and transition functionality, and changed the background of the menu to fit differently in the viewport. Other than that I just used two google web fonts called Montserrat and Open sans. The menu tutorial can be found here: https://www.htmlgoodies.com/beyond/cms/creating-a-hamburger-menu-with-html-css-and-jquery.html.

Other

I used a lot of knowledge from my design classes regarding what users want to see from a website, visual affordances, and user interface design patterns. I definitely expanded my knowledge of jQuery because on the last project I couldn't get the menu working, so the tutorial definitely helped!
